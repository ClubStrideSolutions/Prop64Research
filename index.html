üéØ Cross-Sectional Implications

Youth in Vallejo face compounded disadvantage across every life domain. The concentration of risk in specific census tracts creates "toxic stress" environments where multiple adversities interact synergistically.

Geographic targeting essential: Grade F census tracts show convergence of crime, environmental burden, and service failure. These zones require comprehensive intervention combining environmental cleanup, crime reduction, and intensive youth services.

Single-issue approaches will fail: The interconnection of economic, educational, mental health, and safety challenges requires coordinated multi-system intervention. Prop 64 program must address root causes while providing immediate protective factors.
